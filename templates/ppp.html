Project 1 of 10

Yoti for business

Redefining how a company talks about their products to boost leads and earn trust by businesses

Challenge

The B2B side of Yoti's marketing and website had been struggling to generate traction with potential business partners. It was falling short in showcasing what is unique about the digital verification company, and further user research helped identify that features and benefits weren't tailored to business needs. The B2B area of the Yoti website used generic stock photography and a confusing information structure.

The main challenge of this project was to build Yoti's B2B marketing from the ground up, from being centred around a single product (Yoti Digital ID) to an ecosystem of B2B services, packaged in specific combinations to appeal to governments, banking, crypto, gambling, aviation, and the adult industry.

Role

Alex led the design of a bespoke B2B website with tailored product marketing and a rebrand to make the company stand out in a competitive business environment.

He was responsible for competitor analysis and collaborative sessions with Yoti stakeholders to understand the sales process and needs of potential business partners. Based on workshop insights, Alex created a content strategy for each of the services in Yoti's business portfolio which was the basis for creating navigation, text, visuals, pricing structure and CTAs.

He worked closely with a copywriter to create value propositions and content for the website and a visual designer to help bring the abstract topics alive.

Outcome

The new B2B website showcases Yoti as an approachable provider of intricate digital verification services. It does so in a way that doesn't patronise customers new to the field, in a professional but down-to-earth way, leading with a strong vision to solve business problems.

To better suit a B2B environment, the visual language of Yoti's colourful consumer-focussed brand is transformed into a friendly, professional and clean visual style. It reflects the simplicity the Yoti brand stands for and makes the company unique in a sea of 'techy' and obscure visuals used by competitors in the field.

The information structure of the website is intentionally flat, firmly focussed on laying out details in a one-pager approach that keeps customers engaged and incentivised to get in touch.

Sections are designed as modules, reusable across pages and allowing quick edits. The business-focussed text and visuals created for the website lay the foundation for all of Yoti's future B2B marketing initiatives.

Impact

Testing results showed a clear customer understanding of Yoti's B2B products. The website was rolled out in stages, with the first pages going live seeing a substantial increase of B2B leads to come in with mainly positive feedback. Alex ended his work with Yoti before the project saw full development completion.

SunExpress
relaunch

A redesigned brand experience that increased booking revenue and traveller satisfaction online

Challenge

The budget airline SunExpress was ambitious to boost its revenue substantially. The highly competitive airline industry demanded the company to find ways to stand out and guarantee a great customer experience. The current SunExpress website had multiple issues to be optimised: convoluted navigation, non-responsive layouts, customer dropoffs during the booking process and visuals that didn’t resonate with customers, to name a few.

Role

Alex was responsible for redesigning the brand and establishing a new UI style for SunExpress. He art directed the use of photography and illustrations on the website, illustrated a new friendly icon family and optimised the brand typeface to support multiple languages.

Alex collaborated with a creative director to create the information architecture, and UX of primary user flows of the website and the booking system. He designed wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes that allowed the team to validate decisions.
‍

He designed the UI for 100+ pages, helped bring it to life with interactions and assisted the development process by working closely with a front-end developer team.

Outcome

The relaunch of the online experience is a significant step for the SunExpress brand. It establishes the airline in a contemporary look-and-feel while giving travellers access to handy booking features. The redesign of the website has three main aspects at heart:

The redesign has three major aspects at heart:
  • Discovery. Using smart filters, travellers can customise offers based on interests and budget. An online travel guide provides in-depth inspiration and imagery for SunExpress destinations. Furthermore, the travel guide pages are designed as landing pages for customer conversion and replace numerous standalone SunExpress marketing landing pages.
  • Smooth booking. The booking experience is optimised for ease of use, expandability and performance, using micro-interactions and human-centric design to guide users through the process with confidence.
  • New UI system: No matter if passengers are searching for information, use online services or browse flight offers, the new UI is accessible, responsive and easy on the eyes, giving SunExpress a modern appeal that doesn’t feel ‘budget’ at all.

Impact

The website drastically improved online revenue and customer satisfaction on ‘Trusted Shops’ after it’s launch. The rebrand was rolled out into architectural branding of the SunExpress headquarters and various marketing campaigns.

Project 4 of 10

Lufthansa icon library

A new icon library for interfaces, way-finding and marketing of an iconic German brand

Challenge

Lufthansa is Germany's most esteemed airline with a company history of over 100 years and one of the most iconic brand designs. Many parts of the brand have evolved over the years except the Lufthansa iconography.

Designed by the designer Otl Aicher in the 1960ies, the brands original icon set had minimal uses for today's requirements. Work on visuals for Lufthansa by creative collaborators throughout the years, led to an iconography that's deeply inconsistent and difficult to use with confidence today.

Luthansa required a consistent style for its iconography within the brand guidelines. Furthermore it needed assets for hundreds of topics needed throughout the company and publicise it effectively to creative partners and internal departments.

Role

Alex helped create the icon style and guidelines, working alongside a creative director. He designed, and art directed over 700 icon designs and worked closely with Lufthansa's head of marketing and a partner agency to ensure brand-centric decision-making throughout the project.

Further, he was responsible for the UX/UI of an online library, working closely alongside a front-end developer.

Alex created the concept and visuals for an internal rollout campaign within the company involving the design of print marketing and animations. ‍

‍Outcome

The new iconography is guided by the modern aesthetic of the Lufthansa brand, grounded in 'german understatement', and optimised to be used on websites, mobile apps, inflight entertainment, animations, architectural signage, catering, shopping, aircraft maintenance, med bays and many others.

Over 700 icon designs are part of the icon library at launch. An extensive style guide elaborates on how to design consistent Lufthansa icons for future projects.

A bespoke online tool was developed to give partner agencies and Lufthansa employees easy access to all assets. It enables the download of icons in multiple image formats and brand colours within seconds. Additionally, new icons can be 'ordered' right from within the tool, given the right user permissions.

An internal marketing campaign was designed with bold visuals and a tongue-in-cheek approach to brand communication to generate awareness with employees and creative partners.

Impact

The new iconography made icons for all Lufthansa topics consistent and aligned with the brand identity.

The online tool saw great feedback by Lufthansa's creative partners and internal departments and was used daily to access assets and place new icon orders.

Project 5 of 10

Provemyage

An AI-powered online portal for adult websites that keeps kids out and lets adults in without it getting really awkward

Challenge

ProveMyAge is an answer to the UK's digital economy act, better known as porn block. The controversial law requires all adult websites accessible in the UK to verify the age of its users and prevent minors from accessing the page.

ProveMyAge is a portal developed as a safe and private way to verify peoples age on adult websites. It runs on secure verification technology (of its mother company Yoti} and allows people to prove their age in seconds by using AI-powered age estimation methods or an anonymous digital ID. It does away with giving out credit card numbers, home addresses, photo IDs or phone numbers.

Due to the delicate nature of the topic and brand, it was required to tailor the communication and digital experience of the portal to emphasise trust and reassurance with the sceptical customer base.

Role

Alex led the UX/UI of the web portal and designed a product marketing website in close collaboration with copywriters. He was responsible for casting and directing a video shoot for instructional videos and creating high-fidelity prototypes for testing.

Work was done in collaborative design sprints, working with a user researcher, marketing associates and senior business stakeholders.

Impact

The result of Alex's contribution saw a substantial improvement in qualitative customer feedback and trust towards the ProveMyAge brand in the product perception and on the portal itself with test participants. The project is currently awaiting a launch date; all work is therefore still confidential. To learn more, please get in touch.

SunExpress tutorials

A short film series to tell passengers all about the airlines' travel services

Challenge

The budget airline SunExpress needed to promote the booking services offered on their website, such as booking extra luggage or adding insurance to a trip. Primarily the less tech-savvy passengers hadn't heard of these self-services online or were hesitant to use them.

Role

Alex wrote, and art directed the short animated film. He defined the visual style and designed scenes together with a junior illustrator and worked closely with a motion designer and vocal artist to bring the narrative alive.

Furthermore, he was responsible for the designs of a company-wide rollout campaign, on website visuals, out-of-home ads, inflight-lunchboxes and social media ads.

Outcome

The SunExpress online services are translated into a series of animated short films, making the mundane tasks more engaging and memorable for passengers. Each episode tells a short travel story about using different online services to solve travel issues. The first instalment revolves around the topic of online-check, the next on booking extra luggage, and so on.

The films combine light-hearted storytelling with step-by-step instructions that easy to follow and recall later. A cast of characters adds quirky personality and a backstory to every instalment, incentivising to watch all 'episodes' of their travel adventures.

The design of characters is easy for designers to manipulate to account for the quick turnaround time of the project.

The first instalment of the short films is accompanied by a campaign on social media ads and the website. Besides, the characters are featured on designs of sticker packs, out of home campaigns, SunExpress merchandise and onboard lunchboxes.

Impact

The first episode of the series was dubbed into three languages and watched over 50.000 times on social media channels for Germany and Turkey. The quirky characters resonated in German and Turkish demographics alike. So much that the character design and visual style were featured on various other brand touchpoints and campaigns.

Project 9 of 10

Fadista typeface

Award-winning typeface and deep dive into the history of Portuguese typography

Heading

An award-winning typeface and deep-dive into the history of Portuguese typography

Outcome

Fadista is an eccentric experimental typeface, inspired by the Portuguese fado music and letterings by the artist Stuart Carvalhais, created throughout the 1920ies and 1930ies.

The typeface is the result of meticulous research of music partiture covers from the era, identifying visual characteristics and organising styles into a coherent system to use them as a typeface.

The glyph palette features an excentric family of characters that can change their appearance dynamically through OpenType features, encouraging graphic exploration and playful use of the typeface.

The display typeface supports over 300 languages and features multiple versions of letters, numbers, diacritics, symbols and wordmarks, resulting in over 900 different glyphs.

Fadista strikes a balance between historical origins and contemporary look, making it a living discourse about aesthetics, trends and currentness in graphic design.

Role

Alex was in charge of the typeface design, research and technical development of the font. He designed a printed type specimen, created promotional visuals and animations.

‍Outcome

The typeface won the Art Directors Club Bronze Award (Junior Competition 2014) and DDC (German Designer Club) Award in the future category 2014
